Study of Mathematics

  • 2004: B.A. in Mathematics
  • 2010: M.A. in Mathematics

I exited high school with credit for Calculus 1 & 2, and continued taking math in college. I learned that I have the frustrating inability to let go of a difficult mathematics question. My plans of being a Spanish major fell by the wayside as I realized solving math puzzles is more fun than writing essays. I exited undergrad with a love for mathematics, and decided to become a teacher.

After a few years of teaching high school math, I decided to further my education by getting a Master's in Mathematics. I studied Combinatorics and Graph Theory at the University of Montana. My research covered König-Egerváry graphs, a generalization of bipartite graphs.

Highlights

  • Scored in the 70th percentile on the 2002 Putnam Competition.
  • Talks given to Combinatorics Seminar described by professor as "best we've had this year".
  • Scored 100% on math section of GRE (phew!).
  • Received ETS Recognition of Excellence for performance on the Mathematics section of the Praxis II test for teacher licensure.
